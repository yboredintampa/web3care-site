Privacy

Effective: September 21, 2025

Our stance

We do not store personal health information (PHI) on our servers. PHI remains on your devices, encrypted end-to-end. We design for HIPAA-minded controls, but until we complete formal reviews and vendor BAAs, we do not claim HIPAA certification.

What we store (and where)

  • On your devices: All PHI is encrypted locally (e.g., vitals, meds, notes). You can export or delete it anytime from the app.
  • Backups you control: Optional encrypted backups to a second device, USB/NAS, or a user-owned home box. Keys never leave your devices.
  • On chain (Solana): Roles, session-key metadata, and integrity hashes only—never PHI.
  • Storj/IPFS: Public release artifacts and documentation for transparency; no PHI unless client-side encrypted and kept private.

Messaging & notifications

Daily SMS/email contain no PHI. They link back to the app, which decrypts locally on your device.

Security

  • Client-side encryption by default (modern AEAD; keys stored in device keystore where supported).
  • Seedless sign-in with email 2FA and passkeys (WebAuthn). Device revocation and key rotation are built-in.
  • We do not sell data. We do not share PHI with advertisers, brokers, or analytics providers.

Your choices & rights

  • Export or delete your local data at any time.
  • Choose your own backup destination (or none).
  • Revoke devices and rotate access from within the app.

Analytics

Disabled by default. If enabled later, we’ll use minimal, privacy-respecting metrics and avoid collecting PHI.

Children’s privacy

Web3Care is intended for adults and families managing care. If you believe a child’s data was submitted in error, contact us for assistance.

Incident response

If we ever discover a security issue affecting non-PHI data we handle (e.g., waitlist emails), we will notify affected users and take corrective actions. PHI is stored only on user devices and user-controlled backups.
